## One line

Build a wearable, pet-collar-mounted camera system that automatically captures low-angle, close-up point-of-view photos ("lickface" photos) when your dog or cat approaches a familiar human, syncing them directly to a dedicated Drive folder.

## Problem

Our best friends have a completely different perspective on our homes and lives. We miss out on capturing the genuine, raw reactions and moments of affection from their PoV, and manually triggering cameras disrupts the natural interaction. Also, existing collar cameras require manual sdsdi/uploads, which often lands in a local dead-end vault.

## Proposal

Refine a lightweight, collar-mounted raspberry pi zero or eSIM-based camera module that utilizes a proximity sensor (or BLE tracking) to detect when the pet is within 3-feet of a familiar human (like Brian or Sarah). When triggered, it captures a 3-second burst of high-resolution photos. If the home Wi-Fi is reachable, or over a local shared link, it automatically uploads the best shots to `drop_pile/from_bunny/lickface/`
