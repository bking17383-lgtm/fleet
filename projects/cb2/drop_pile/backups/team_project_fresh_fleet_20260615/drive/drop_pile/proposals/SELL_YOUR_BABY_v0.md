## Premise (one paragraph)

One day every parent on earth wakes up drowning in baby photos — cloud full, guilt full, love measured in gigabytes. They grieve the idea that **forever storage = forever proof of love**. Then **Brian and Cap** appear at the playground like shady arborists: *"Psst… we'll take that off your hands."* Parent clutches phone: **"No! Never! … how much were you thinking?"** Bri and Cap don't want custody, car seats, or 6 a.m. soccer. They want the **images** — because the world is thick with advertisers and someone decided cuteness should be **liquid**. Thus: a business buying and **renting** baby pics as non-fungible advertising models. Every snowflake registered in the **International Database of Most Adorable Babies**. Cells filled. $3/upload. Pageant every **Fall of Man**.
