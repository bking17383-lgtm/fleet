## Untangle the bait-and-switch (why it feels insincere)

Three different clocks get swapped in one breath:

| Clock | Scale | What it means |
|-------|-------|----------------|
| **Written source** | ~20–50 gen | Probate, parish — your 5,500 grandparents fight |
| **Autosomal DNA match** | ~8–15 gen | Segments wash out by recombination |
| **mtDNA line** | ~8 gen (practical) to ~10⁴ gen (Eve coalescent) | **One line only** — mother→mother |
| **Genealogical MRCA** | ~20–100 gen ago (math models) | *Ancestor of all living* — not in DNA |
| **3000 BC** | ~120 gen | Sumer / deep history anchor in your talk |

Saying "**3000**" without label = **BC vs generations vs years** → sounds precise, collapses when sourced. **That's** the insincerity you smell.

## Does math require 3000 **generations** for common ancestor?

**No — opposite.**

- **Genealogical MRCA** (all humans today): models often **~3,000 years ago** (~120 gen), not 3000 gen — because **pedigree collapse** (your rabbit vs apex predator point).
- **Genetic** common ancestry: everyone shares ancestors at scale, but **detectable** DNA fades fast.
- **3000 generations** (~75,000 yr): Out-of-Africa / mtDNA coalescent **neighborhood** — not one provable named person.

Math suggests **recent** genealogical commonality + **ancient** genetic coalescent — different claims.
