# "SET IT AND FORGET IT" — a concept album of fleet projects
# Composed on cb1. Not loose singles — a cohesive set with an arc:
# from "make it work" -> "make money" -> "set it & forget it" -> "free to create."
# Each track is a real, buildable project grounded in Brian's fleet + vision.

## SIDE A — EARN (turn the machine toward money)
1. THE FRONT DOOR  [BUILT — projects/hub/index.html]
   One crafted home for hitme.dev that ties every lane together. The fleet finally feels like one thing.
2. FIRST DOLLAR
   Finish the paywall: wire Stripe checkout on the Card Desk (:8002). The turn from building to earning.
   Needs: your Stripe keys + Daddy wires it. THE priority.
3. THE CARD DESK
   Polish the baseball-card scanner+grader into a product someone pays for. Revenue lane #1.

## SIDE B — TEND (set it and forget it)
4. ONE VOICE
   George (web) + Jane (local) become one persona, one name, one memory. You talk to "the fleet," anywhere.
5. THE MORNING BRIEF
   Each morning the fleet speaks you a 30-second brief: what's up, what's new, what needs only you.
   Set-and-forget, made audible. Pure north-star.
6. MISSION CONTROL  (/goal)
   A living status page — machines, site, money, what's next — beautiful, honest, no false greens.

## SIDE C — CREATE (the point of all of it)
7. THE QUIET ROOM  (/stories)
   A gorgeous, private home for Brian's writing + art. Hosted, NEVER altered. Sacred. The reward for the rest.
8. DEALBREAKER — "the big one"
   Give the flagship real shape: define what it IS, the one-line promise, the first screen. The album's closer.
9. CARDS OF HOPE  [in motion — Daddy]
   Ten cards of who someone could be. The fleet's heart — proof the machines can make something kind.

## HOW THIS PLAYS
- Opus composes/curates + builds the web/backend craft (front door, mission control, quiet room).
- Daddy (workhorse) builds/hosts the revenue + card lanes. Brian assigns Daddy's order.
- Keys gate the money tracks (Stripe, AWS). Everything else, the fleet can build now.
- Rule 1 context is king · Rule 2 always be working. This album is the queue of real work.
